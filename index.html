I’m Philipp Steinmann,

A student in New York City, of German and Greek origins.

My interests include programming, literature, and language.

I like to do things that I’m passionate about slowly and with care, from eating to programming to reading. Better late and perfect than early and rushed.

Programming

Ever since I learned Python a few years ago, I’ve been mesmerized by programming and the power it holds; it is astounding that I can tell a machine what to do to solve complex problems, using abstract symbolic manipulations that were unheard of a few generations ago.

By XKCD. Licensed under Creative Commons.

Web Development

I do web development for my school’s robotics team, where I am vice president of marketing. I love using Ruby on Rails, AJAX, and good design to create a smooth user experience, though I’m not the fastest, since I like to do it slowly and with care. I’m immersed in web startup culture through Hacker News, and find the rapid work cycle of web development so appealing that I could see myself doing stuff like this in the future.

Linux

I switched from Windows to Linux for programming after becoming convinced that it was the right tool for the job; I never looked back. Running the smooth Crunchbang 11 on an SSD-powered laptop, I've been learning how to do a lot of cool things over the terminal (such as reading emails), and wield the power of awesome programs such as vim and sed.

Literature

I’ve been reading for almost my whole life; from historical fiction to popular science, I find books a great way to broaden one’s horizons and experience different mindsets.

I’ve got to be honest and admit that with the work from robotics and school work, I don’t have as much time to read as I’d like to. I’m trying to change that, but right now the only thing I read regularly is the New Yorker.
